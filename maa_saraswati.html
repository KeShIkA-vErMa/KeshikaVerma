Maa Sarasvati: The Goddess of Knowledge

Maa Sarasvati, the Hindu Goddess of knowledge, is the first among the holy trinity of the divine feminine. The name Sarasvati stems from the Sanskrit root “saras”, meaning “that which is fluid.” This signifies her origins as a river goddess during the ancient Vedic period.

The Goddess is regarded as the patron of knowledge, poetry, literature, and music. For centuries, seekers of wisdom and learning have sought her divine blessings.

She is typically portrayed as a serene woman with four arms, seated upon the Shweta Padma (white lotus), a symbol of purity and transcendence. Each of her four arms signifies a fundamental aspect of human personality: the mind, intellect, ego, and consciousness.

In her hands, she carries a book (symbolizing knowledge), and a veena (highlighting her deep connection with music). Her white garments denote purity and elegance.

Among Hindus, the colour yellow is considered especially dear to the Goddess. This shade represents joy, ecstasy, and hope — qualities that echo the spirit of spring. During this season, bright mustard blossoms fill the fields, and life is renewed after the cold of winter.

Thus, every year between February and March, India celebrates Basant Panchami — the arrival of spring and a tribute to Maa Sarasvati. On this auspicious day, she is honoured as the embodiment of learning in all its forms: from the sciences to the arts and music.

May her blessings guide us towards wisdom, creativity, and enlightenment.
