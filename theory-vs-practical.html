Theory vs Practice: Rethinking How We Learn

“The greater aim of education is not knowledge, but action.” — Herbert Spencer

Education is an important and crucial part in the journey of our life. Through this article, I want to share my views regarding the ways of education.

Education is a road to a bright future and a good life ahead. That’s why today, everyone wants to pursue quality education. If our primary education is good, it will undoubtedly benefit our future. In today’s world, there are two aspects of education: practical and theoretical.

As a student, I have always been made to focus on the importance of theoretical knowledge. ICSE focuses on an elaborate syllabus and in-depth understanding. This theoretical learning has both pros and cons. It definitely makes Indian students extremely hardworking. However, I strongly believe that practical learning is crucial—it allows us to think beyond the theories we’ve memorized.

Practical education leads to innovation and a deeper understanding of fundamentals. If you look at successful people, top executives, and entrepreneurs—they aren’t always school toppers. They’re often people who know how to apply theory into practice.

As Elon Musk once said, “Most colleges are just meant for fun, not learning, because the learning is just paper-filling activity.” That doesn’t mean we stop studying—it means we need an interactive, hands-on approach to what we learn.

Some might think I’m just rambling, but reality hits when even a graduate from a top Indian college like IIT has to work for 19 years to afford a Lamborghini (on average). No one wants that. What changes the game? Skills you master early—through practical knowledge.

Practical education means gaining knowledge through experience. It’s how the real “success stories” are written. It makes learning enjoyable, memorable, and more effective. And no—it’s not limited to computers or business; practical knowledge fits every field.

Students who learn this way are more confident and have better chances of landing jobs—or building their own ideas. If you ask successful people, they’ll always emphasize practical skills.

Theoretical knowledge, on the other hand, is short-lived. Without application, we forget it within a couple of months. That’s why action and execution matter more.

“Find something that looks like work to others but feels like play to you.” — Naval Ravikant

So choose wisely. Study deeply—but learn actively. Because real learning sticks when it’s lived, not just read.
