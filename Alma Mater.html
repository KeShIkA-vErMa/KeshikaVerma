Alma Mater

In Kindergarten, as a wide-eyed child stepping into the school gates, filled with a mix of excitement, curiosity, and a touch of nervousness, I embarked on a journey of growth, discovery, and transformation. Each day held the promise of new friendships, exciting adventures, and captivating lessons that sparked my imagination. I was given innumerable opportunities throughout my school life to reach my true potential and prove myself.

Now, as I bid farewell to my beloved alma mater, Our Lady of the Snows School, I am reminded of the profound transformations that have taken place within me during my school life. Ralph Waldo Emerson once said, 'The mind, once stretched by a new idea, never returns to its original dimensions.' My school journey has been a testament to this quote, as I have experienced a remarkable expansion of knowledge and perspectives.

Alongside the academic lessons, my school life has taught me invaluable life lessons and allowed me to forge lifelong friendships. The collective experiences shared with my classmates have shaped me into a more compassionate, resilient, and well-rounded individual. Through the ups and downs, the challenges and the triumphs, I have discovered my true capability and learned to embrace change as a catalyst for personal and intellectual growth.

The remarkable teachers, with their profound knowledge and unwavering commitment, have not only imparted wisdom but also instilled a love for learning within me. They believed in my potential, challenged me to surpass my own expectations, and nurtured my individual strengths. The experience of being the head girl instilled in me a sense of pride, gratitude, and a deep appreciation for the trust bestowed upon me.

As I embark on new adventures beyond the walls of my alma mater, I carry with me the cherished memories, enduring friendships, the impact of my teachers and principal, and the invaluable lessons learned through leadership, that have made me better equipped to navigate the world. On the first day of my school, little did I know that the same wide-eyed and nervous individual, stepping into those school gates for the first time, will leave them for the last feeling prepared to face the future with confidence, resilience, and a commitment to making a positive difference in the world.

Thanks, Alma Mater!
